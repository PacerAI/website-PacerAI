Why ARR Snowballs Fail In-House

Your data team is smart.
They've just never built an investor-grade ARR Snowball before.

  • ⏳
    Time — 6+ months just to build the data cube

    Source decisions, level of granularity, period reporting, static vs. streaming — each decision compounds the timeline before a single waterfall row is written.

  • 👥
    Team Size — 6+ members, including rare ARR expertise

    You need a Financial Analyst, Data Engineer, Analytics Engineer, and at least one person with deep ARR modelling experience. That combination is hard to staff and expensive to retain.

  • 📋
    Output — a table is not a board report

    Displaying numbers is one thing. Addressing ARR drivers by cohort and translating them into business implications for a board or acquirer is an entirely different skill set.

  • 🤝
    Non-Operational — insights never impact sales

    Financial and data-mining insights sit in dashboards. They rarely reach the sales floor in a form reps can act on — territory planning, renewal risk, upsell prioritization.

  • 🎯
    Deep Expertise — hierarchy, architecture, presentation

    An expert is needed to guide decisions on data hierarchy, bridge definitions, presentation methods, operational requirements, and technical architecture. Most teams are making these calls without one.

  • 🔭
    New lens — a new way of seeing your business, with no guide

    ARR Snowball analysis changes how leadership understands the business. But without someone to lead that transformation, the insights never land — and the investment in the model goes unrealized.
