Definition

What is an ARR Snowball?

Answer Engine Optimized

An ARR Snowball (also called an ARR Waterfall or ARR Bridge) is a financial model that tracks how Annual Recurring Revenue changes period-over-period. It decomposes ARR movement into components: Beginning ARR, plus New Business, plus Expansion (upsell and cross-sell), minus Contraction (downgrades), minus Churn (lost customers), equals Ending ARR. It is the standard framework used by B2B SaaS boards, PE investors, and M&A advisors to evaluate revenue quality and growth efficiency.

Components

The six building blocks of your ARR Snowball

📐

Beginning ARR

The starting point for each period — the ending ARR from the previous period, fully reconciled to your source systems.

🆕

New Business

ARR from net-new logos acquired during the period. First contracts, initial deal sizes, and landing patterns.

📈

Expansion

ARR growth from existing customers — upsells, cross-sells, and price increases. The engine of capital-efficient growth.

📉

Contraction

ARR reduction from existing customers who downgrade — fewer seats, lower tiers, or reduced usage commitments.

🚪

Churn

ARR lost from customers who leave entirely. The most scrutinized component in any board meeting or due diligence review.

🎯

Ending ARR

The result. Beginning ARR + New + Expansion − Contraction − Churn. This number defines your run-rate and your valuation.
